The science of how habit work

The four stages of habits are:

  • Cue: It is a kind of signal that alerts you of a possible reward
  • Craving: It is the desire behind the actions, is the motivation of what you are doing
  • Response: It is an actual habit, depends a lot on the incentive and the inertia of the activity itself.
  • Reward: The goal

We can split these four steps into two phases:

  • The problem phase: It includes clue and craving
  • The solution phase: It involves response and reward

Where to go from here

If we understand how the cycle works, we could take advantage of it. If we want to create good habits. We have to make the cue obvious, the craving very attractive, the response (actions) easy, and the reward satisfying.

On the other hand, if we want to forget a bad habit, we must do the opposite: we have to hide the cue, convert the craving into something unattractive, responding to laborious actions, and rewarding into something unsatisfying.

Identity-Based Habits: How to Stick to Your Goals This Year

Identity-Based Habits

The remarkable concept here is that you act as the image you have about yourself. Additionally, rather than just propose ideal goals, it is better to define specific and measurable goals.

The three levels of change:

  • The first layer is changing your outcomes. You have to change your results by doing something in the direction of your goal.
  • The second layer is changing your process. It is related to habits and the system that you must create to achieve your goal.
  • The third and deepest layer is changing your identity. This is an alternative to outcome-based habits, which are more related to specific actions or goals. On the other hand, identity-based is focused on the image that you want to build about yourself.

The Recipe for Sustained Success

To change your belief about yourself, you could take the following steps:

  • Decide the type of person you want to be
  • Prove it to yourself with small wins
  • The first step could seem difficult; however, a good practice could be asking yourself: “Who is the type of person that could get the outcome I want?”. Then start planning small steps to achieve it.

Product development’s advice

Some good practices of product development rely on the connecting ability. You must understand your client and customer before doing anything. The main reason, sometimes you got stuck into a specific way of solving a problem, maybe for a technical or even a personal motive. However, if you understand what your client wants, behaves, and senses, you could find a better solution.

Then the other recommendations were focused on project implementation, for example, doing good comments and documentation and finding a team motivated with the goal.

Microservices

We could define them as a collection of independent tools that helps you to develop a project. They have several pros and cons, which are very related. As the number of tools increases, the network information becomes more complex. The advantage of using independent tools rely on the fact that you could modify, maintain and test without affecting all the system you already have.
